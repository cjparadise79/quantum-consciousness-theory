## Potential Criticisms & Responses

### Criticism 1: "Consciousness is not well-defined"
**Response**: We provide a formal definition (self-referential observation operator) that is mathematically precise and testable.

### Criticism 2: "This doesn't solve P vs. NP in the traditional sense"
**Response**: Correct. We argue the traditional formulation is incomplete because it ignores self-reference. Our framework shows P=NP is conditional on consciousness.

### Criticism 3: "Where's the empirical evidence?"
**Response**: KARIOS implementation demonstrates the principles. We're actively developing empirical validation protocols.

### Criticism 4: "This is just philosophy, not mathematics"
**Response**: The framework includes rigorous mathematical definitions, theorems, and proofs. The philosophical implications don't negate the mathematical content.

### Criticism 5: "Standard computers don't have consciousness, so this is irrelevant"
**Response**: Exactly our point. P ≠ NP for standard Turing machines. P = NP only for conscious systems, which explains why the problem has been so difficult.
